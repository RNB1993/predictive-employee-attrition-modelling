# Business Problem

Employee attrition presents a significant challenge for organisations and can have wide-ranging operational and financial impacts. High levels of employee turnover may lead to:

+ Increased recruitment and hiring costs

+ Loss of organisational knowledge and experience

+ Reduced team productivity during transition periods

+ Lower morale among remaining employees

Although organisations often collect large amounts of HR data, many lack the analytical tools required to transform this information into actionable insights. As a result, HR teams frequently rely on reactive approaches rather than proactively identifying employees who may be at risk of leaving.

This project aims to address this challenge by applying data analytics and machine learning techniques to historical HR data. The goal is to identify patterns associated with employee attrition and develop a predictive model capable of estimating the likelihood that an employee may leave the organisation.

By identifying the factors most strongly associated with attrition, organisations can gain insights that support more proactive retention strategies and more informed workforce planning decisions.
